Vow Writing Tips

Getting Started

  • Start by listing the qualities you love most about your partner.
  • Think about your favorite shared memories and what they mean to you.
  • Consider what promises you want to make for your future together.

Structure

  • Open with a declaration of love or a meaningful anecdote.
  • Share what your partner means to you and how they have changed your life.
  • Make specific promises that reflect your relationship.
  • Close with a heartfelt statement about your future together.

Practical Tips

  • Aim for 1-2 minutes in length (roughly 150-300 words).
  • Practice reading your vows aloud several times before the ceremony.
  • It is okay to be emotional -- have a backup copy with the officiant.
  • Coordinate with your partner on tone and length, but keep the content a surprise.

Inspiration

  • Reflect on the moment you knew this was the person you wanted to marry.
  • Think about challenges you have overcome together.
  • Consider incorporating a meaningful quote, song lyric, or inside joke.
  • Write from the heart -- authenticity matters more than perfection.
