Seating Group Tips

Church / Religious Groups: Seat church friends together, especially if they don't know many other guests. They'll feel more comfortable.
Work Colleagues: Group coworkers together but consider mixing departments if they're already friendly across teams.
College Friends: These groups often have strong bonds. Seating them together usually creates the most fun tables.
Extended Family: Keep family branches together. Cousins who haven't seen each other in years appreciate reconnecting.
Mixed Groups: If someone belongs to multiple groups, the algorithm picks the table where they'll have the most connections.
Priority Levels: Set higher priority (8-10) for tight-knit groups like close friend circles, lower (3-5) for looser associations like "neighbors."
